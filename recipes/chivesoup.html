Chive Soup (Canh He)

Description

Chive soup is one of my favorite soups! I don't know why, but when I'm asked what's my favorite soup, I think of this! The ingredients are simple and you can omit ground pork or dried shrimp (one of either, not both). Tofu is also optional. This dish can be vegan, but I have tried many ways in the past and they weren't good, so I would stick with adding some pork and/or shirmps to elevate the broth.

This recipe shows you how to make a simple chive soup.

Ingredients

  • Chives (a lot, usually the garlic chives are sold in greater quantity and cheaper at the Asian groceries)
  • Ground pork (marinate with salt, pepper, sugar, MSG)
  • Dried shrimps, a handful
  • Soft tofu, cut into blocks
  • Garlic, minced
  • Onion or green onions (white part), minced
  • Salt, pepper, MSG, fish sauce

Steps

  1. Clean and cut chives into ~5cm strips
  2. Soak the dried shrimps in warm water for at least 10 minutes
  3. Add oil to a heated pot on medium heat, then add onions and garlic, fry until aromatic
  4. Add the ground pork, partly cook the pork
  5. Add water, as much as you like, but remember to leave some space for the chives to go in later. Season with 1 tbsp of salt, and some MSG
  6. Add in the tofu, cook until the water boils
  7. Add in the chives and turn off heat
  8. Season the broth with fish sauce, pepper to taste

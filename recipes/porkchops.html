Pan-fried Porkchops

Description

This is a simple recipe with many different variations in marinating. It's better with pork shoulders, but I usually buy pork loin or tenderloin from Walmart. This recipe includes ingredients and instructions on how to make a simple pan-fried porkchops.

This dish pairs well with rice, fresh vegatbles, and Vietnamese simple pickled radish and carrots and an optional egg. In addition, a simple bowl of bone broth soup helps complete the meal.

Ingredients

I have done this so many times that I don't remember or sure the exact measurement... so good luck!

  • Pork (tenderloin, loin, shoulder, butt, etc)
  • Minced lemongrass or Green onions (white part)
  • Spices (soy sauce, MSG, fish sauce, pepper, sugar, etc)
  • Mushrooms (sliced)
  • Onion (sliced into strips)

Steps

  1. Slice pork into slices, about 1-1.5 cm thick, then pound them
  2. In a large bowl, mix in the pork, lemongrass (or green onion, NOT both), a little bit of soy sauce, MSG, sugar, garlic and a little bit of fish sauce
  3. Let the meat marinade for a few hours. If you don't have time, 30 minutes to an hour is fine
  4. Heat oil in a pan on medium heat, add in the pork pieces and cook until the meat is cooked
  5. When the pork is almost cooked, add in chopped mushrooms, sliced onions and cook until the mushroom and pork cooked
  6. Lower the heat, add a tbsp or two of soy sauce, 2 tsp of sugar, and some water.
  7. Flip the pork so that both side is moist. Cook until the water is reduced then turn off the heat
  8. Plate the food and top it off with green onions and some cracked pepper

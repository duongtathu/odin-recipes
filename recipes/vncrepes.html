Banh Xeo (Vietnamese Savory Crepes)

Description

I didn't appreciate Banh Xeo until after high school, now I eat it pretty regularly. I associate Banh Xeo with the late afternoons, early evenings when I went on a 15-12 minutes moped ride with my mom to a faraway Banh Xeo restaurant with her coworkers and my best friend. It was always fun back then when my world was stable, and happy.

This Vietnamese crepe, like many Vietnamese dishes has a lot of vegetables. It is similar to any basic savory crepe, but with many fresh herbs and vegetables. However, I don't include mung bean filling here because I don't know how to make it, and I usually just make a quick and simpe version of this recipe... as demonstrated below.

Ingredients

  • Vietnamese crepe/pancake mix yes...I bought the mix, it's easier)
  • Ground pork
  • Shrimp
  • Bean Sprouts
  • chives
  • Onions
  • Fish sauce
  • salt, pepper, oil, MSG, siracha, lemon or juice
  • taro roots
  • carrots
  • garlic
  • chili peppers

Steps

  1. Prep:
    • Mix pancake mix with water or coconut milk according to instructions
    • Cook ground pork in a pan with some oil (marinate pork with salt and pepper if you wish)
    • Boil or pan-fry shrimps
    • Wash and rinse bean sprouts
    • Wash chives and chop into 5cm strips
    • Cut taro root, and carrots into strips then parboil them
    • Chop onions into strips or small cubes
    • Prepare other fresh herbs
    • mince garlic and chili peppers
  2. Cook:
    1. Heat oil in a pan on medium heat
    2. When oil is hot, add the pork and shrimp
    3. Then pour in enough crepe mixture into the pan so that there is a thin layer of batter on the surface of the pan
    4. Take a handfull of chives and bean sprouts and put it in the middle of the batter. You can crack some salt or sprikle some MSG at this stage
    5. Close the lid of the pan and let it cook/steam for about 3 minutes
    6. Open the lid, and fold the crepe in half, then close the lid again, let it cook for another 3 minutes
    7. Meanwhile, mix about 4 tbsp of water, 2 tbsp sugar, 2-3 tbsp fish sauce, minced garlic, minced red chilli peppers, 1 tbsp lemon or lime juice, and some siracha into a bowl. This is your dipping sauce, adjust to taste
    8. Open the lid, flip the crepe and let it cook for a minute or two then you're done!
  3. Plate: Assemble the crepes with herbs and vegetables such as cilantro, lettuce, cucumbers, and Thai basil. In addition, Vietnamese simple pickle radish and carrots pairs well with the dish but it's optional, if you have it, you can also add it to the dipping sauce to flavor the sauce more.

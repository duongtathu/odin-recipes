Stir-fried Kohlrabi

Description

Stir-fried Kohlrabi is one of my favorite side dish, it's just a perfect crunchy and savory taste that I really enjoy. No one could make it as good as my grandmother's. Later, I found out that the secret ingredient is just... more sugar. I still make this dish from time to time, and sometimes, I shed a tear or two eating this because it reminds me of her.

In addition to kohlrabi, this dish includes carrots, garlic and green onions. Carrots are optional. Actually, you can customize this dish by adding some shrimps, ground pork, or eggs for some proteins, the meat will surely elevate the dish to the next level. However, this dish can also be just as simple as stir-frying garlic with kohlrabi and some spices.

Ingredients

I usually eyeball the ingredients for this recipe... so I don't have the precise measurements here.

  • 1 Kohlrabi
  • 1/2-1 Carrot
  • Garlic
  • Green onions
  • ground pork or shrimp
  • 2 tbsp corn starch
  • Cooking oil, sugar, MSG, pepper, oyster sauce, soy sauce

Steps

  1. Prep:
    • Peel kohlrabi skin and carrot skin, chop into strips
    • Chop the green part of green onions into strips and cut the white parts into small pieces
    • Mince garlic
    • If you use meat, prep the meat and marinate it with a little bit of salt and pepper and green onions or shallots
    • Mix 2tbsp of cornstarch with 4 tbsp of cold or room temperature water
    Mince Garlic. If you use meat, prep the meat.
  2. Cook:
    1. Add oil to the heated pan
    2. When the oil is hot, add the chopped white part of the green onions and garlic
    3. Until the aromatics smell good, add the meat and stir-fry until it's cooked or almost cooked.
    4. Remove the meat and leave the oil in the pan, keep the heat on
    5. Add the kohlrabi and carrot into the pan and stir-fry
    6. Meanwhile, season with some oyster sauce and/or soy sauce, a sprinkle of MSG, 1 tsp or more of sugar, and pepper to your liking
    7. When the vegetables are almost cooked, add the corn starch mixture, and the meat, keep going until they are all cooked, then turn off the heat
  3. Plate: You can add some cilantro or a little chilli peppers on top with some more cracked pepper. Enjoy!

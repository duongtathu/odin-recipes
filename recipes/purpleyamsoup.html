Grated Purple Yam Soup (Canh Khoai Mo)

Description

This recipe gives instructions on how to make a simple grated purple yam soup.

This soup is one of my favorites! It's different from most Vietnamese soup because it has a thicker texture. However, the soup can be thin and more liquidy if you add more water, it's yummy either way.

Ingredients

  • Grated purple yam (hand grated or store bought)
  • Ground pork, as much as you want, at least a small handful
  • Dried shrimp
  • Garlic, minced
  • 1 Sharlot or 1/4 onion, minced
  • Seasonings: salt, pepper, fish sauce, MSG
  • Neutral oil

Steps

  1. Soak dried shrimp in warm water for at least 10 minutes, discard the water when use the shrimp
  2. In a pot, heat the pot and add oil
  3. Add minced garlic and onion/shallot when the oil is hot. Stir fry until aromatic
  4. Add ground pork and dry shrimp, season with some salt, pepper and MSG
  5. Add in water, as much as you want, and 1 tbpsp of salt
  6. When the water boils, add grated purple yam and 1 tsp of sugar
  7. Cook until the water boils again, then turn off the heat
  8. Add some fish sauce, adjust seasoning to taste
  9. Top it off with green onions, cilantro and some more cracked peppers and the dish is done!
